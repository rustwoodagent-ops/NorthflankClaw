# Singing Fundamentals Cheat Sheet

## Purpose
**Casual singing gives expression. Deliberate practice gives progress.**
Use this sheet to bring shape, attention, and quality to your daily singing without turning it into pressure.

---

## Before You Start
- Stand tall, knees soft, ribs easy, jaw loose.
- Decide **one focus** for this session.
- Sip water.
- Start below full volume.
- Aim for **clean reps**, not heroic effort.

---

## 1-Hour Practice Flow
### 1) Warm-up — 10 min
- Gentle hums, lip trills, sirens, light scales
- Wake up breath, resonance, and ease
- Keep it comfortable; do not push for range early

### 2) Technique — 15 min
Pick **1–2 technical goals** only:
- breath steadiness
- pitch accuracy
- vowel clarity
- smoother register shifts
- lighter onset / less throat tension

### 3) Song Work — 20–25 min
- Work on **1–2 songs**, not seven
- Sing in short sections
- Repeat with a purpose: rhythm, pitch, tone, phrasing, diction
- Do one “performance pass” only after fixing details

### 4) Targeted Repair — 10 min
Find the exact trouble spot and isolate it:
- one line
- one interval
- one vowel
- one breath point
- one rhythm pattern

Slow it down. Fix it. Rebuild it. Reinsert it into the song.

### 5) Cool Down + Notes — 5 min
- Light hums / easy descending sounds
- Note:
  - what improved
  - what still feels unstable
  - what to focus on tomorrow

---

## The Pillars: Fast Checks
### Posture
**Look for:** tall, balanced, released

**Off signs:** collapsed chest, lifted chin, locked knees, tight shoulders

### Breath
**Look for:** low, quiet inhale and steady airflow

**Off signs:** gasping, shoulder lifting, running out too early, pushing sound

### Pitch
**Look for:** centred notes and confident intervals

**Off signs:** sliding into notes, going flat at phrase ends, guessing leaps

### Tone
**Look for:** clear, easy, consistent sound

**Off signs:** pressed, breathy, dull, or strained tone

### Diction
**Look for:** clear words without jaw or tongue tension

**Off signs:** muddy consonants, swallowed vowels, tight mouth

### Rhythm
**Look for:** steady pulse and accurate entries

**Off signs:** rushing, dragging, unstable phrasing, late starts

### Vocal Health
**Look for:** ease, recovery, and no lingering irritation

**Off signs:** scratchiness, pain, fatigue, loss of top notes, needing force

---

## Quality Practice Rules
- Practise with **one intention at a time**.
- Stop repeating the same mistake mindlessly.
- If it gets worse, reduce volume, range, or speed.
- Work in short sections before full runs.
- Record brief clips when needed; listen for facts, not feelings.
- Finish with a note, so tomorrow starts with direction.
