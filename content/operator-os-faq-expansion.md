## Frequently Asked Questions

### What exactly is Rustwood Operator OS?

A practical operating system for founders and solo operators who want less chaos, better decisions, and stronger execution. It's not another productivity app — it's a coherent framework for organizing your work, making clearer decisions, and actually moving forward on what matters.

### Who is this for?

Founders, solo operators, AI power users, and anyone building something who feels like they're operating in fragments. If you have the ambition and the opportunity but feel scattered, this is for you.

### What's included?

Six core modules:
1. **Operator Command** — Priorities, planning, focus
2. **Execution System** — Daily rhythm, triage, leverage
3. **AI Workflow** — Routing, decisions, structure
4. **Business Assets** — Content conversion, authority building
5. **Monetisation Readiness** — Offers, CTAs, conversion
6. **Review & Self-Correction** — Continuous improvement

Each module includes templates, frameworks, and practical tools.

### What formats are available?

- Notion workspace (importable template)
- Google Workspace (Sheets, Docs)
- PDF printables
- Markdown source files

Use what works for your workflow.

### How is this different from [Notion/Obsidian/Asana/etc.]?

Those are tools. Operator OS is a system. Tools give you places to put things. Systems give you clarity about what to work on, when to work on it, and how to know when it's done. You can use Operator OS with any tool — or across multiple tools.

### How do I get access when it launches?

Early access subscribers get first notification. Join the list on this page.

### Can I share this with my team?

Personal licenses are for individual use. Team licenses will be available at launch for multiple seats.

### What's the "AI Workflow Layer" about?

Practical frameworks for using AI without losing control. Includes task routing templates, AI vs Human decision matrices, workflow mapping, and a prompt library — so you can use AI intentionally, not accidentally.

### Is this just another productivity course?

No. This is an operating system — templates, frameworks, and tools you implement, not content you consume. Less video watching, more doing.

### What if I'm already using [specific tool/stack]?

Operator OS is tool-agnostic. The frameworks work with Notion, Obsidian, Airtable, Google Workspace, or plain text files. Use what you already have.
