Quick Practice Sheet

Singing Fundamentals

Casual singing gives expression. Deliberate practice gives progress.

Before you start: Stand tall, knees soft, ribs easy, jaw loose. Pick one focus. Sip water. Start below full volume. Aim for clean reps, not heroic effort.

1) Warm-up — 10 min

  • Hums, lip trills, sirens, light scales
  • Wake up breath, resonance, and ease
  • Do not push for range early

2) Technique — 15 min

  • Pick 1–2 goals only
  • Breath steadiness
  • Pitch accuracy
  • Vowel clarity / smoother register shifts

3) Song Work — 20–25 min

  • Work on 1–2 songs, not seven
  • Sing in sections
  • Repeat with a purpose
  • Do one full pass after fixing details

4) Targeted Repair — 10 min

  • Find one exact trouble spot
  • Slow it down
  • Fix it cleanly
  • Put it back in context
5) Cool Down + Notes — 5 min
Light hums / easy descending sounds. Write what improved, what still feels unstable, and what to focus on tomorrow.
Posture
Tall, balanced, released.
Off: collapsed chest, lifted chin, tight shoulders.
Breath
Low, quiet inhale and steady airflow.
Off: gasping, pushing, running out early.
Pitch
Centred notes, confident intervals.
Off: sliding in, flat endings, guessed leaps.
Tone
Clear, easy, consistent sound.
Off: pressed, breathy, dull, strained.
Diction
Clear words without tension.
Off: muddy consonants, swallowed vowels.
Rhythm
Steady pulse and accurate entries.
Off: rushing, dragging, late starts.
Vocal health: no pain, no forcing, recover well.
Quality practice rules
  • Practise with one intention at a time.
  • Stop repeating the same mistake mindlessly.
  • If it gets worse, reduce volume, range, or speed.
  • Work in short sections before full runs.
  • Finish with a note so tomorrow starts with direction.
