## No jargon. No hype. Just what you need to know.

# Run AI on Your Own Computer
## Keep your data private, avoid subscription fees, and understand exactly what's happening.

### Value Proposition Bullets

**Set A (Clarity Focus):**
- **Finally understand** what "local AI" actually means without the technical lecture
- **Learn the real trade-offs** between cloud and local AI (privacy, cost, capability)
- **Get a simple decision framework** to know if local AI makes sense for you

**Set B (Control Focus):**
- **Keep your data completely private**—nothing leaves your computer
- **Stop paying monthly fees** for AI tools you can run yourself
- **Own your AI setup** and control exactly how it works

**Set C (Practicality Focus):**
- **20 pages, not 200**—quick to read, faster to act on
- **Zero technical background required**—explained like you're five (but respectfully)
- **Concrete examples** of what local AI can and can't do for you

## Page Layout Recommendations

### Local AI in Plain English Page

**Recommended Structure:**
1. **Headline:** Option B ("Confused About Local AI?")
2. **Subhead:** Keep the subhead that comes with Option B
3. **Bullets:** Set C (Practicality Focus)
4. **CTA:** CTA Set 1 (Low Friction)
5. **Social Proof:** "Join 500+ people who finally understand local AI"
6. **Preview:** Show first page or table of contents screenshot

**Rationale:** The audience for this guide is likely early in their AI journey and feeling overwhelmed. The "confused" headline meets them where they are. Practical bullets reduce anxiety about complexity. Low-friction CTA respects that they're not ready to commit yet.

### Your First AI Workflow Page

**Recommended Structure:**
1. **Headline:** Option A ("Build Your First AI Automation This Afternoon")
2. **Subhead:** Keep the subhead that comes with Option A
3. **Bullets:** Set B (Simplicity Focus)
4. **CTA:** CTA Set 2 (Outcome-Focused)
5. **Visual:** Show the workflow diagram or n8n screenshot
6. **Social Proof:** "Join 300+ people who built their first automation"
7. **FAQ:** Address "Do I need to code?" and "How much does it cost?" upfront

**Rationale:** This audience wants to build something—they're past awareness and ready for action. The "this afternoon" headline creates urgency and achievability. Simplicity bullets remove the biggest objection ("I can't do this"). Outcome-focused CTA reinforces what they'll achieve.
