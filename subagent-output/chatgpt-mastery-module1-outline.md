## Lesson 1: The Problem with How Most People Use ChatGPT (3 min)

### The Default Mistake

Most people open ChatGPT and type something like:

> "Write me a blog post about productivity"

Then they're disappointed with generic, bland output. They conclude "ChatGPT isn't that good" or "This doesn't work for my needs."

**The truth:** ChatGPT can produce excellent results. But like any tool, you need to know how to use it.

### Why Default Prompts Fail

| What most people do | Why it fails |
|---------------------|--------------|
| Vague requests | AI has no context about your needs |
| No format specified | Results are random structure |
| No constraints | Output is too long, too short, or wrong tone |
| Single attempt | First draft accepted without refinement |

### The Shift: From User to Director

Think of ChatGPT as a skilled but inexperienced assistant. They can do great work—but only with clear direction.

**Weak direction:** "Write something about productivity"

**Strong direction:** "I'm a freelance designer who struggles with time management. Write a 300-word email to my clients explaining my new project timeline process. Tone: professional but warm. Include: why I'm making this change, what the new process looks like, and a reassurance that quality won't suffer."

Same tool. Dramatically different result.

### What Changes Today

By the end of this module, you will:
- Have a simple framework for every prompt
- Know the one setting that changes everything
- Have written 3 improved prompts
- See immediate improvement in your outputs

## Lesson 2: The One Setting to Change Immediately (2 min)

### Custom Instructions

ChatGPT has a feature called **Custom Instructions** that most people ignore. This is a mistake—it's the single highest-impact setting for improving your results.

**What it does:** Tells ChatGPT who you are and how you want responses formatted—automatically, in every conversation.

### How to Set It Up

**Step 1:** Open ChatGPT settings
- Click your name (bottom left)
- Select "Custom instructions"

**Step 2:** Fill in "What would you like ChatGPT to know about you?"

Example for a marketing professional:
```
I'm a marketing manager at a B2B SaaS company. 
My audience is small business owners who aren't tech-savvy.
I prefer concise, actionable advice over theory.
I often need content for LinkedIn, email newsletters, and blog posts.
```

Example for a consultant:
```
I'm a management consultant who works with mid-sized companies.
I need frameworks, structured analysis, and professional communication.
My clients value clarity over cleverness.
I often need to explain complex concepts simply.
```

**Step 3:** Fill in "How would you like ChatGPT to respond?"

Example instructions:
```
- Be concise. Get to the point quickly.
- Use bullet points for lists and steps.
- When giving advice, explain why it works.
- If you're unsure about something, say so rather than making it up.
- Use professional but conversational tone—not academic or overly casual.
```

**Step 4:** Enable for new chats
- Toggle "Enable for new chats" ON
- Click Save

### Why This Matters

Without custom instructions, every conversation starts from zero. You waste time establishing context.

With custom instructions, every conversation starts with your context already loaded. Results are instantly more relevant.

**Exercise:** Take 5 minutes now. Write your custom instructions. Use the examples above as templates, but make them specific to your situation.

## Lesson 3: The 4-Part Prompt Framework (5 min)

### The Formula

Every effective prompt follows this structure:

```
[Context] + [Task] + [Format] + [Constraints]
```

Let's break down each part.

### Part 1: Context

**What:** Background information the AI needs to understand your situation

**Examples:**
- "I'm preparing a presentation for senior executives..."
- "I'm a freelance writer pitching to a new client..."
- "I know nothing about this topic and need beginner-level explanation..."

**Why it matters:** Context shapes everything. The same task produces different output for a CEO vs. a student vs. a technician.

### Part 2: Task

**What:** Exactly what you want the AI to do

**Strong verbs to use:**
- Write, draft, create
- Summarize, condense
- Analyze, evaluate
- Compare, contrast
- Explain, teach
- Brainstorm, generate ideas
- Rewrite, rephrase

**Weak vs. strong task statements:**
- Weak: "Tell me about marketing"
- Strong: "Create a 3-month content marketing plan for a new accounting firm"

### Part 3: Format

**What:** How you want the output structured

**Format options:**
- Bullet points
- Numbered steps
- Table or comparison
- Email format
- Report with headings
- Script or dialogue
- Specific length (words, paragraphs, pages)

**Example:** "Format as a table comparing the three options, with columns for cost, time required, and difficulty."

### Part 4: Constraints

**What:** Limitations, requirements, or boundaries

**Common constraints:**
- Length ("Keep under 200 words")
- Tone ("Professional but not stiff" / "Casual and friendly")
- Audience ("Explain as if to a 10-year-old" / "Use industry terminology")
- Things to include ("Mention our sustainability commitment")
- Things to avoid ("Don't use jargon" / "No buzzwords")

### Full Example

**Weak prompt:**
> "Write an email about the project delay"

**Strong prompt (4-part framework):**
> "I'm a project manager at a software company. [CONTEXT]
> 
> Write an email to our client explaining that their project will be delayed by two weeks. [TASK]
> 
> Format as a professional business email with: opening, explanation of reasons, what we're doing to minimize impact, new timeline, and closing reassurance. [FORMAT]
> 
> Keep it under 200 words. Be honest about the delay but emphasize our commitment to quality. Avoid making excuses. [CONSTRAINTS]"

## Lesson 4: Your First Three Prompts (10 min)

### Exercise 1: Rewrite a Weak Prompt

**Original (weak):**
> "Help me with my resume"

**Your task:** Rewrite using the 4-part framework

**Example rewrite:**
```
[CONTEXT] I'm a mid-level marketing professional with 5 years of experience, currently job hunting for a senior role at a tech company.

[TASK] Review my resume summary and suggest improvements to make it more compelling for senior marketing positions.

[FORMAT] Provide: 1) What's currently working, 2) Specific changes to make, 3) A rewritten version I can copy.

[CONSTRAINTS] Keep the tone confident but not arrogant. Focus on results and leadership potential. Maximum 100 words for the rewritten summary.
```

### Exercise 2: A Prompt for Your Actual Work

Think of something you do regularly. Draft a prompt using the 4-part framework.

**Template:**
```
[CONTEXT] 

[TASK] 

[FORMAT] 

[CONSTRAINTS] 
```

**Examples to get you started:**
- Drafting emails to clients
- Summarizing meeting notes
- Creating social media content
- Explaining technical concepts to non-technical people
- Brainstorming ideas for a project

### Exercise 3: Quick Win — Try It Now

**Pick one of these and run it:**

**For managers:**
```
I'm preparing for a difficult conversation with an employee who's been underperforming. 

Draft a script for opening this conversation that: sets a constructive tone, addresses the performance issue directly but kindly, invites their perspective, and establishes next steps.

Format as talking points I can reference, not a word-for-word script.

Keep it professional and supportive. Avoid accusatory language. About 150 words.
```

**For content creators:**
```
I need to create a week's worth of LinkedIn posts about productivity for busy professionals.

Generate 5 post ideas. For each, provide: a hook/opening line, the main point, and a call-to-action.

Format as a numbered list with clear headings for each post idea.

Keep hooks under 15 words. Make them practical, not motivational fluff. No hashtags needed.
```

**For consultants:**
```
I need to explain the concept of "customer churn" to a client who's new to business metrics.

Explain what churn is, why it matters, and give 2-3 examples of how to reduce it.

Format: Start with a simple analogy, then the formal definition, then the examples.

Use plain English—no jargon. Keep it under 200 words. Make it reassuring, not alarming.
```

## Lesson 5: Immediate Improvement Checklist

### Before Your Next ChatGPT Session

- [ ] Custom instructions set up (Lesson 2)
