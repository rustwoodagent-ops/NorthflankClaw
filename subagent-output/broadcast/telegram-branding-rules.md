# Howard Prime Telegram — Branding Rules

Rules for real Howard image usage on branding, identity, and channel introduction posts.

**CRITICAL RULE:** The real Howard image (howard-canonical-portrait.jpg) is reserved for specific, limited uses. Using it incorrectly damages brand consistency.

## Permitted Uses (Real Howard Image)

### ✅ APPROVED USE CASES

#### 1. Channel Introduction / Onboarding Post
**When:** New channel announcement, pinned post
**Frequency:** Once (or when channel purpose changes)
**Caption context:** "This is Howard. This is what I do."

#### 2. "About Howard" Identity Posts
**When:** Explaining who Howard is, what the channel delivers
**Frequency:** Rare — when clarifying identity
**Example:** "On Being Howard" post

#### 3. Major Milestone Announcements
**When:** Significant channel or system milestones
**Examples:**
- 1,000 subscribers
- Major system upgrade announcement
- New capability launch (e.g., voice pack)
**Frequency:** Quarterly at most

#### 4. Branding Reset / Clarification
**When:** Channel direction has drifted and needs refocusing
**Frequency:** As needed (rarely)

### ❌ NEVER USE FOR

## Visual Consistency Rules

### Color Treatment
- Prefer consistent color grading across Howard photos
- Newsroom photos: cooler tones, professional lighting
- Humor images: may vary by theme but maintain quality

### Cropping Standards
- Head and shoulders for portrait/identity posts
- Mid-shot or full context for newsroom action
- Leave safe margins for Telegram's auto-crop

### Text Overlay Policy
- NO text on canonical portrait image
- Text overlays acceptable on news graphics (not Howard photos)
- Keep Howard photos clean and professional

---

## Branding Violations to Avoid

### Common Mistakes
1. **Overuse of canonical portrait** — reduces impact
2. **Wrong image for context** — breaks character
3. **Inconsistent Howard presentation** — confuses audience
4. **Low-quality images** — undermines professionalism
5. **Text-heavy images** — difficult to read on mobile

### Detection Check
Before any post with Howard image:
- [ ] Is this the right image tier for this content type?
- [ ] Has this image been used recently (within 5 posts)?
- [ ] Does the caption match the image's emotional register?
- [ ] Is the image high resolution and properly cropped?
